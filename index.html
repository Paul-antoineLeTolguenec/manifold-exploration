01

Smooth Manifolds

A manifold is a space that locally resembles Euclidean space. We formalize this with charts, atlases, and smooth transition maps.

Definition

A topological manifold of dimension n is a second-countable Hausdorff space where every point has a neighborhood homeomorphic to an open subset of \mathbb{R}^n.

Charts and Atlases

A chart (U, \varphi) maps an open set U \subset M to \mathbb{R}^n. An atlas is a collection of charts covering the entire manifold.

\varphi_\beta \circ \varphi_\alpha^{-1} : \varphi_\alpha(U_\alpha \cap U_\beta) \to \varphi_\beta(U_\alpha \cap U_\beta)

Smooth Structure

When all transition maps are smooth (C^\infty), the atlas defines a smooth structure, turning a topological manifold into a smooth manifold.

02

Tangent Spaces

At each point of a manifold, the tangent space captures all possible directions of movement — a local linear approximation to the curved space.

Definition

The tangent space T_pM at a point p \in M is the vector space of derivations on germs of smooth functions at p.

Tangent Vectors as Derivations

A tangent vector v \in T_pM acts on smooth functions f: M \to \mathbb{R} via directional derivatives.

v(f) = \sum_{i=1}^n v^i \frac{\partial (f \circ \varphi^{-1})}{\partial x^i}\bigg|_{\varphi(p)}

The Tangent Bundle

The disjoint union of all tangent spaces TM = \bigsqcup_{p \in M} T_pM forms the tangent bundle, itself a smooth manifold of dimension 2n.

03

Connections & Christoffel Symbols

To differentiate vector fields on a manifold, we need a connection — additional structure beyond the smooth manifold itself.

Definition

An affine connection \nabla assigns to vector fields X, Y a new vector field \nabla_X Y, satisfying linearity and the Leibniz rule.

Christoffel Symbols

In local coordinates, the connection is encoded by the Christoffel symbols:

\nabla_{\partial_i} \partial_j = \Gamma^k_{ij} \, \partial_k

Covariant Derivative

The covariant derivative of a vector field Y = Y^k \partial_k along X = X^i \partial_i:

\nabla_X Y = X^i \left( \frac{\partial Y^k}{\partial x^i} + \Gamma^k_{ij} Y^j \right) \partial_k

Geodesics & Parallel Transport

Geodesics are the “straightest possible” curves on a manifold — they parallel-transport their own tangent vector.

Definition

A curve \gamma(t) is a geodesic if its tangent vector is parallel-transported along itself: \nabla_{\dot\gamma} \dot\gamma = 0.

Geodesic Equation

In local coordinates, the geodesic equation is a system of second-order ODEs:

\frac{d^2 x^k}{dt^2} + \Gamma^k_{ij} \frac{dx^i}{dt} \frac{dx^j}{dt} = 0

Parallel Transport

A vector field V along \gamma is parallel if \nabla_{\dot\gamma} V = 0. This defines a linear isomorphism between tangent spaces along the curve.

05

Curvature

Curvature measures how a manifold deviates from being flat. The Riemann tensor captures this intrinsic property completely.

Definition

The Riemann curvature tensor measures the failure of covariant derivatives to commute:

R(X, Y)Z = \nabla_X \nabla_Y Z - \nabla_Y \nabla_X Z - \nabla_{[X,Y]} Z

Ricci and Scalar Curvature

Contracting the Riemann tensor yields the Ricci tensor R_{ij} = R^k_{\ ikj} and scalar curvature R = g^{ij}R_{ij}.

Sectional Curvature

For a 2-plane \sigma = \text{span}(X, Y) in T_pM:

K(\sigma) = \frac{\langle R(X, Y)Y, X \rangle}{\langle X, X\rangle\langle Y, Y\rangle - \langle X, Y\rangle^2}

06

Riemannian Metrics

A Riemannian metric turns a smooth manifold into a geometric space where we can measure lengths, angles, and volumes.

Definition

A Riemannian metric g is a smooth assignment of an inner product g_p: T_pM \times T_pM \to \mathbb{R} to each tangent space.

Length and Distance

The length of a curve \gamma: [a, b] \to M and the geodesic distance:

L(\gamma) = \int_a^b \sqrt{g_{\gamma(t)}(\dot\gamma(t), \dot\gamma(t))} \, dt

Levi-Civita Connection

Every Riemannian manifold has a unique torsion-free, metric-compatible connection. Its Christoffel symbols are determined entirely by the metric:

\Gamma^k_{ij} = \frac{1}{2} g^{kl} \left( \frac{\partial g_{jl}}{\partial x^i} + \frac{\partial g_{il}}{\partial x^j} - \frac{\partial g_{ij}}{\partial x^l} \right)

Projection onto Manifolds

Given a point in ambient space, finding the closest point on a manifold is fundamental for optimization, statistics, and ML.

Definition

The projection map \pi: U \to M sends each point in a tubular neighborhood to its nearest point on M.

Normal Bundle and Tubular Neighborhoods

For an embedded submanifold, the normal bundle provides directions perpendicular to the manifold. A tubular neighborhood is a diffeomorphic image of a neighborhood of the zero section.

Exponential Map

The exponential map \exp_p: T_pM \to M sends tangent vectors to the manifold along geodesics:

\exp_p(v) = \gamma_v(1) \quad \text{where} \quad \gamma_v(0) = p, \; \dot\gamma_v(0) = v

08

Manifold Learning

When data lies on or near a low-dimensional manifold in high-dimensional space, manifold learning algorithms recover that structure.

The Manifold Hypothesis

High-dimensional data often concentrates near a low-dimensional manifold. This assumption underpins dimensionality reduction and modern representation learning.

Classical Algorithms

ISOMAP approximates geodesic distances via graph shortest paths. Locally Linear Embedding (LLE) preserves local linear reconstructions. Laplacian Eigenmaps use the graph Laplacian to find smooth embeddings.

UMAP and t-SNE

Modern methods optimize embeddings by matching neighborhood structures between high-dimensional and low-dimensional representations.

09

Latent Space Geometry

Neural networks learn latent spaces that carry rich geometric structure. Understanding this geometry connects differential geometry to deep learning.

Pullback Metrics in Autoencoders

A decoder f: \mathcal{Z} \to \mathcal{X} induces a Riemannian metric on latent space via the Jacobian:

g_{ij}(z) = J(z)^T J(z), \quad J(z) = \frac{\partial f}{\partial z}

Geodesics in Latent Space

Under the pullback metric, straight-line interpolations in \mathcal{Z} are replaced by geodesics that respect the learned geometry — producing smoother and more meaningful transitions.

Connections to Representation Learning

Disentangled representations correspond to coordinate systems where the metric tensor is diagonal. The Fisher information metric on statistical manifolds bridges information geometry with neural network training.
